The Athlone Town team of 1895

Athlone Town Football Club is the oldest senior football in Ireland.

Founded in 1887 by Orlando Robert Coote, the Club joined the then Free State League in 1922; the first club from outside Dublin to do so.

The Club won the Free State (FAI) Cup in 1924, beating Cork side Fordsons 1-0 in the Final on St. Patrick's Day 1924. The side did not concede a single goal in the competition, and beat Shelbourne and Bohemians on their way to the Final.

In recognition of their achievements, five members of the Cup winning team were chosen to represent the fledgling state at the 1924 Olympics in Paris, thus becoming Ireland's first international footballers, and Olympians. This period is chronicled by Tadhg Carey in his book "When We Were Kings".

The Club dropped out of senior football in 1929, and didn't return to it again until 1969, when they re-joined the League alongside Finn Harps.

There wasn't long to wait for more drama. After finishing runners-up to Bohemians in the 1974/75 season, Town qualified for the UEFA Cup.

Drawn against little knock Norwegian side Valerenga, Athlone advanced to the next round 4-2 on aggregate.

If their First Round opponents were little known, their Second Round opponents were world renowned AC Milan.

Athlone welcomed the Italian giants to St. Mel's Park on a September afternoon in 1975, and sent shockwaves around the world when they emerged with a 0-0 draw.

In fact, Athlone missed a penalty, when John Minnock's weakly-struck spot-kick were gratefully gathered by Italian international goalkeeper Enrico Albertosi.

League titles followed in 1981 and 1983 under the guidance of local legend, Turlough O'Connor.

In recent years the Club has fallen from grace, spending most of their time in the lower, First Division.

However, there was some cheer in 2013, when controversial manager Roddy Collins guided the side to the First Division title, and promotion to the Premier Division.

FAI Cup Winners: 1924

League Champions: 1980/81, 1982/83

League Ruuners-Up: 1974/75

League Cup Winners: 1979/80, 1981/82, 1982/83

League Cup Runners-Up: 1983/84, 1999/00

Tyler All-Ireland Cup Winners: 1979/80

Tyler All-Ireland Cup Runners-Up: 1980/81

Leinster Senior Cup Winners: 1969/70, 1987/88, 1991/92

Did You Know?

Athlone Town have had 4 home grounds; The Sportsground, The Ranelagh Grounds, Saint Mel's Park and Athlone Town Stadium

The Club moved to Athlone Town Stadium in 2007.
